About me...

Events

February 2021

1st Latin American Quantum Computing Forum

I organized an event 100% in Spanish with three objectives: fostering collaboration in the Latin American quantum computing community, promoting quantum computing education, and strengthening the links between industry and academia around quantum computing.

The event consisted of two half-days of talks and break-out rooms, where the first day was focused on industry and the second one was focused on academia. We had panelists from Oakridge National Lab, Quantum South, Quantum Hispano, EAFIT, Hood College, North Carolina State University, Qureca, and IBM. We received excellent feedback from the attendees, both for the talks and the break-out rooms. The break-out rooms were a success in allowing the participants to discuss topics around quantum computing and foster a sense of community.

We had 540 registrations in total with 282 attendees the first day and 149 attendees the second day.

August 2017

1st Makeathon Colombia

The 1st Makeathon Colombia was an event created to promote student creativity and involvement for solving problems related to agriculture with technology. We created this event in a way that would be fun and engaging for the students participating, while also contributing to solve real problems.

We had to plan every aspect of the event, from getting the space, materials, communications, speakers, timeline, sponsors, food, shirts, visit to a farm, and much more. We invited students from different universities and we were fully booked, with 60 students from 8 universities in Colombia, France and Germany.

The event was a complete success. The sponsors proposed different problems, but the students were free to choose a problem of their own too. At the end of the 4 days all groups had functioning prototypes of their solutions, and one group was invited by the problem’s sponsor to continue the development of the solution.

Education Projects

August and September 2020

Cloud for Everyone

Along with 2 colleagues I co-created a series of workshops about Cloud Computing for all employees of IBM Colombia. We realized that IBM was trying to position itself as a leader in hybrid cloud, but many employees had never even accessed the IBM Cloud. We decided to act upon this by designing a series of workshops that could be taken by anyone in the company, regardless of their technical knowledge. To make the courses complete, we included 30 minutes of theory and 1 hour of practice per workshop. The first time we opened registrations for the series of 4 workshops, the entrees sold-out in a couple of hours. Given the success of the first round of workshops we opened a new round, and were also fully booked in a matter of hours. The waitlist was of about 300 people. For the first series of workshops we had an Net Promoter Score (NPS) of 80, and by incorporating the attendees’ feedback we managed to increase it to 85 for the second round. Due to these workshops, my colleagues and I were awarded the Growth and Entrepreneurship Mindset distinction for the third quarter of 2020 at IBM Colombia. In order to make these workshops accessible to more people we are using a design thinking methodology to build the workshops into a virtual course that can be taken on demand.
